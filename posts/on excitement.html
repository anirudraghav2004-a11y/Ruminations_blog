On Excitement

Life as an adult sucks. i'm interning right now at a law firm. why does it suck? i suspect it's because it's not what i imagined it would be. a glamorous place, with a retinue of associates frantically rushing from room to room at a flashy partner's service, assisting with some critical research that will turn the odds of a case. that's the impression (devastatingly misleading one, i might add) that a series like suits gave me. it wasn't to be. life as an adult is slow - it's unexciting, repetitive and bureacratic. In the law firm, not everything you do will involve a juicy doctrinal question - far from it, most will involve isolated, drab queries on what filing to do or summarizing board resolutions or redmarking contracts. exciting work seems like a chimera, a mirage - something you chase. but i suppose there's a universal truth about everything that's exciting: it's always exciting until you do it. i love cricket. when I play a match, I give it my all. sometimes, I can't sleep the night before in excitement. but when I step into the match, the excitement retires, as it were. movies: i look forward to watching movies. but the moment I step into a theater, the passions abate. i wonder why. it's probably because part of what makes events exciting in life is your anticipation of what it would be. you have an ill-formed, amorphous, high-level idea of what it's going to be. you haven't worked out the details; you might not even know what you're expecting. but you know you're expecting something really good, something enamouring. but when the realities strike, you're often left disappointed. but when you reflect on the disappointment, you can't quite put your finger on what was missing. excitement is at once what makes things in life worthwhile but also what lets you down. this is characteristic of the human condition. the best and worst things in life are the same thing. Love - you love at the peril of a devastating, void-inducing loss. Ambition - you aspire at the risk of losing everything if you don't attain your goals. Once you hit rock bottom, an elaborate scheme of cope ensues.
